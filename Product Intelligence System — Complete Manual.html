Chapter 1 — What Is This System

The Problem

You run a health and wellness import business. You need to find trending products before they peak and before your competitors notice them. Manually checking TikTok, Reddit, Amazon, and Google Trends every day is unsustainable. By the time you notice a trend, it may be too late to source.

The Solution

The Product Intelligence System automates the entire trend detection pipeline. Every night, it scrapes 12 platforms, scores each product using a weighted 4-job formula, convenes an AI Research Council to deliberate, fires alerts when signals change, and sends you a daily email summary. You wake up, read one email, and know exactly which products are worth investigating.

How It Works at a High Level

  1. Scrape — Automated agents collect data from Reddit, TikTok, Instagram, Amazon, Google Trends, and more. Each platform has a dedicated scraper that runs nightly.
  2. Score — A 4-job scoring engine combines all platform signals into a single composite score (0–100). Each job measures a different aspect of product demand.
  3. Deliberate — Five AI agents analyze the data independently and vote Buy, Watch, or Pass. Dissenting opinions are surfaced, not suppressed.
  4. Alert — When scores cross thresholds, verdicts change, or concerning patterns emerge, alerts fire immediately.
  5. Report — A daily email arrives with scores, intelligence summaries, platform highlights, and actionable insights.

A Typical Day

At 1:00 AM, the pipeline starts. By 4:00 AM, all scrapers have run, scores are updated, the Research Council has deliberated, and your daily email is sent. When you check your inbox around 8–9 AM, you see a complete intelligence report. If something needs attention, you open the dashboard and drill into the product scorecard.

Who Should Use It

  • Victor (Owner) — Full access. Makes final sourcing decisions. Can override the Research Council. Manages formula weights and system configuration.
  • Analysts — Can view all data, manage products, track brands/competitors, and log sourcing decisions. Cannot modify the scoring formula or system settings.
  • Viewers — Read-only access to the leaderboard, discovery feed, posts, council verdicts, and alerts. Good for partners who need visibility without control.

What Decisions It Helps Make

  • Which products are trending up and worth investigating?
  • Is a trending product a fad or a lasting opportunity?
  • Are real people buying, or is it just social media noise?
  • What’s the sentiment among actual buyers on Amazon?
  • When is the right time to source — too early, in the window, or too late?

Chapter 3 — The Scoring System

The 4-Job System

Every product is evaluated across four independent jobs. Each job answers a different question about product demand.

Job 1 — Early Detection (30% weight)

Question: “Is this product generating organic social buzz?”

PlatformWeight within JobWhat It Measures
TikTok40%View velocity, engagement per video, comment intent, creator tier
Instagram30%Engagement per post, comment intent, content volume, caption intent
YouTube15%Long-form reviews, tutorial demand (pending activation)
X (Twitter)10%Real-time buzz, news-driven spikes (pending activation)
Pinterest5%Save rates, demographic reach (pending activation)
TikTok sub-score: vel_norm * 0.50 + eng_norm * 0.30 + comment_intent * 0.15 + creator_tier * 0.05 Instagram sub-score: engagement * 0.45 + comment_intent * 0.25 + volume * 0.20 + caption_intent * 0.10

Job 2 — Demand Validation (30% weight)

Question: “Is there real, sustained consumer demand beyond social media hype?”

PlatformWeight within JobWhat It Measures
Google Trends45%24-month slope, year-over-year growth, breakout detection, seasonal patterns
Reddit35%Sentiment, velocity, purchase intent score, discussion volume, buy-intent bonus
Facebook20%Community group demand (pending activation)
Reddit sub-score: sentiment * 0.25 + velocity * 0.20 + intent * 0.20 + volume * 0.25 + intent_bonus * 0.10 Google Trends sub-score: slope_norm * 0.40 + yoy_norm * 0.40 + lasting_bonus (20 if not breakout)

Job 3 — Purchase Intent (25% weight)

Question: “Are people actually buying this product?”

PlatformWeight within JobWhat It Measures
Amazon50%BSR trend, review velocity, rating score, satisfaction score, repeat purchase signals
Etsy30%Handmade vs mass market ratio (pending activation)
Walmart20%Mass market demand validation (pending activation)
Amazon sub-score: bsr_trend * 0.35 + review_velocity * 0.25 + rating * 0.20 + satisfaction * 0.15 + repeat * 0.05

Job 4 — Supply Readiness (15% weight)

Question: “Can you actually source this product at viable margins?”

PlatformWeight within JobWhat It Measures
Alibaba100%Supplier count, MOQ trends, unit pricing, competing suppliers

Supply readiness matters because a trending product with no reliable supplier is not actionable. A product with many suppliers competing on price suggests good sourcing conditions.

The Coverage Penalty

The coverage penalty exists because incomplete data should produce lower confidence, not inflated scores.

coverage_ratio = active_jobs / 4 coverage_penalty = 0.5 + (coverage_ratio * 0.5) adjusted_score = raw_score * coverage_penalty
Active JobsCoveragePenaltyEffect on Raw Score of 70
1 of 425%0.625x70 → 43.8
2 of 450%0.75x70 → 52.5
3 of 475%0.875x70 → 61.3
4 of 4100%1.0x70 → 70.0

This means a product needs data from at least 3 jobs to have a realistic chance of reaching Watch (55), and typically needs all 4 jobs active to reach Buy (75).

Chapter 4 — The Platforms

Reddit

Why it matters: Reddit is where consumers have honest, long-form discussions about products. Unlike social media where content is promotional, Reddit threads contain genuine opinions, complaints, and purchase considerations.

Two-Pass Architecture

  • Pass 1 (macrocosmos/reddit-scraper, $0.50/1K) — Keyword search across all of Reddit. Finds posts mentioning the product. Filters by relevance score (product name in title = 0.8, keyword in body = 0.2). Keeps top posts by upvotes.
  • Pass 2 (trudax/reddit-scraper-lite, $3.80/1K) — Fetches full comment threads from the top 20 posts. Every comment is scored for intent (L1–L5), sentiment (-1 to +1), and classified as buy-intent, problem-language, or repeat-purchase.

What the signals mean

SignalWhat It Tells You
Mention countHow many posts discuss this product. 50+ in a niche = strong signal.
Sentiment (-1 to +1)Average opinion. Above 0.2 = positive. Below -0.2 = community has concerns.
Avg intent scoreHow close to purchase the community is. Above 0.3 = active consideration.
High intent countNumber of L4+L5 comments. These are people actively buying or about to buy.
Buy intent mentionsComments containing explicit purchase language (“just bought,” “ordered”).
VelocityWeek-over-week change in discussion volume. Positive = growing interest.
Good signal: 58 posts, sentiment +0.15, 134 buy-intent comments, velocity rising. Community is actively discussing and purchasing.
Concerning signal: 20 posts, sentiment -0.35, velocity declining, high problem-language count. Community is reporting issues with the product.

Google Trends

Why it matters: Google Trends is the most reliable demand signal because it represents actual search behavior, not social media noise. A product with sustained GT growth over 24 months is far more likely to be a lasting opportunity than one with a viral TikTok moment.

Key signals

SignalExample ValueWhat It Means
Slope (24-month)+0.0057Average weekly increase in search interest. Above 0.003 = meaningful growth. Above 0.01 = strong growth.
YoY Growth125%How much more search interest this year vs last year. Above 50% with positive slope = confirmed trend.
Breakout FlagfalseWhether GT marked this as a sudden spike. False = organic growth, not a flash-in-the-pan.
Seasonal Pattern“variable”Whether the product has seasonal demand patterns (e.g., sunscreen spikes in summer).

The GT sub-score gives a 20-point bonus for not being a breakout — the system rewards lasting trends over sudden spikes.

TikTok

Why it matters: TikTok is the earliest signal for consumer trends. Products go viral on TikTok before they show up in Google search or Amazon sales. However, TikTok virality alone doesn’t guarantee purchase intent — many viral products are entertainment, not purchase-driven.

Two-Pass Architecture

  • Pass 1 (clockworks/tiktok-scraper, $3.00/1K) — Searches hashtags (product name with spaces removed). Returns up to 200 videos. Filters to videos with 10,000+ views, keeps top 20 by engagement.
  • Pass 2 (clockworks/tiktok-comments-scraper, $5.00/1K) — Fetches up to 50 comments per video from the top 20. Each comment scored for intent and sentiment.

What the signals mean

SignalWhat It Tells You
View velocityViews per post. 500K+ per video = viral reach. Scored via log scale.
Engagement per videoAverage likes + comments per video. 5,000+ = strong engagement.
Creator tier scoreMix of creator sizes. Mega (>1M followers) = 0.90, Macro (>100K) = 0.75, Micro (>10K) = 0.50, Nano = 0.20. Higher = more credible endorsements.
Comment intentAverage intent score from Pass 2 comments. Above 0.2 = meaningful purchase signals in the comments.

Instagram

Why it matters: Instagram confirms visual product discovery. Products performing well on Instagram alongside TikTok indicate cross-platform traction, not just single-platform virality.

Two-Pass Architecture

  • Pass 1 (apify/instagram-hashtag-scraper, $2.30/1K) — Searches hashtags. Returns up to 200 posts. Filters to posts with 20+ engagement (likes + comments), keeps top 20.
  • Pass 2 (apify/instagram-comment-scraper, $2.30/1K) — Fetches up to 50 comments per post. Each comment scored for intent and sentiment.

Limitation: The hashtag scraper does not return follower counts, so creator tier score defaults to 0.5 (neutral).

Amazon

Why it matters: Amazon is purchase confirmation. When a product has 100K+ reviews with 4.5+ stars and 90% satisfaction, you know real people are buying and happy. This is the strongest signal for sourcing decisions.

Two-Pass Architecture

  • Pass 1 (junglee/amazon-crawler, $4.00/1K) — Keyword search with up to 30 results. Filters by min review count (default 500), sorts by reviews, keeps top 10.
  • Pass 2 — Fetches product detail pages for the top 10 ASINs. Extracts BSR (when available), rating distribution (5-star through 1-star percentages), and total ratings count.

What the signals mean

SignalWhat It Tells You
Review countTotal reviews across top products. 50K+ = massive confirmed buyer base.
Review velocityNew reviews since last run. Positive = people are still buying.
Average ratingStar rating across top products. 4.5+ = buyers are satisfied.
Satisfaction scoreWeighted distribution: 5★*1.0 + 4★*0.75 + 3★*0.5 + 2★*0.25 + 1★*0.0. Above 85 = very happy buyers.
1-star percentageProportion of negative reviews. Above 15% triggers a warning.
BSR trendBestseller rank direction. “Rising” = sales accelerating. “Declining” = sales slowing.
Warning sign: If 1-star reviews increase by 3%+ week over week, the system fires an alert: “Negative reviews increasing — quality issue possible.” Investigate before sourcing.

Phase Gating

Each phase depends on the previous one succeeding:

  • If Wave 1 fails, Wave 2 still runs (independent data)

  • Email always sends regardless of upstream failures (reports what data exists)

Chapter 7 — The Research Council

The Research Council is a panel of 5 AI agents that independently analyze each product and vote Buy, Watch, or Pass. They use Claude Sonnet (claude-sonnet-4-20250514) with a 45-second timeout per agent.

The 5 Agents

AgentRoleWhat It AnalyzesWhat It Looks For
Trend ArchaeologistPattern recognition expertHistorical data patterns, GT slope, social velocity curvesProducts showing organic growth patterns similar to past winners. Looks for the “hockey stick” inflection point.
Demand ValidatorConsumer behavior expertIntent scores, sentiment, comment qualityWhether social buzz translates to real purchase intent. Separates genuine demand from noise and hype.
Supply Chain AnalystWholesale sourcingSupplier count, MOQ, pricing, competitionWhether the product can be sourced profitably. Evaluates supplier landscape and margin viability.
Fad DetectorContrarian analystVelocity vs sustainability, breakout flagsWhether the trend will last 6+ months or flame out. Looks for warning signs of unsustainable hype.
Category StrategistLifecycle timing expertPhase classification, category saturationWhether the timing is right. Is the category growing, mature, or saturated? Is there room for a new entrant?

How Voting Works

  • Round 1: All 5 agents independently analyze the data and cast a vote with written reasoning.
  • Round 2 (if split): When votes are split (e.g., 3-2), agents enter a debate round where they respond to dissenting arguments.
  • All-abstain: If no agent feels confident enough to vote, the verdict is “insufficient_data.”

Reading the results

  • Unanimous Buy (5-0-0): All agents agree this is a strong opportunity. Strongest possible signal.
  • Majority Buy (3-1-1 or 4-1-0): Most agents positive, but read the dissent carefully — the contrarian may see a risk others missed.
  • Split (2-2-1): Genuinely uncertain. This is a Watch product — wait for more data.
  • Unanimous Pass (0-0-5): All agents agree this is not a good opportunity. Strong avoid signal.

Victor Override

As the Owner, you can submit your own vote that carries 1.5x weight. Use this when:

  • You have industry knowledge the AI doesn’t (e.g., you know a competitor just exited this category)
  • You’ve sourced similar products before and know the margin structure
  • You disagree with the Council’s assessment based on information not in the data

Your override is logged and contributes to the learning system. Over time, the formula adjusts to align with your judgment.

Chapter 8 — Making Sourcing Decisions

What the System Is Not

  • Not a guarantee. A Buy verdict means strong signals, not guaranteed profit.
  • Not a replacement for your judgment. The system identifies demand — you evaluate viability.
  • Not accounting for your specific constraints (capital, warehouse space, existing inventory, supplier relationships).

What the System Is

  • A signal aggregator that saves you hours of manual research every day.
  • A trend detector that spots opportunities before they become obvious.
  • A confidence builder that tells you when multiple independent data sources agree.
  • A time saver that filters out noise and surfaces only actionable opportunities.

Decision Framework

ScenarioAction
Score 75+ (Buy) AND 4/4 jobs activeInvestigate seriously. All signals align. Check suppliers, margins, and competition. This is the strongest signal the system produces.
Score 55–74 (Watch) AND 3/4 jobs activeMonitor closely. The signal is promising but not fully confirmed. Wait for the missing job’s data and 2–3 more pipeline runs.
Score 55–74 (Watch) AND 2/4 jobs activeInsufficient data. The raw score may be high but coverage is too low. Activate more scrapers and wait.
Score below 55 (Pass)Not yet. Either the signals are weak or there isn’t enough data. Revisit in 30 days.

Pre-Sourcing Checklist

Before sourcing any product the system recommends, verify these 10 items outside the system:

  1. Supplier verification — Do you have a reliable supplier with quality samples?
  2. MOQ feasibility — Can you meet the minimum order quantity?
  3. Margin analysis — Landed cost vs selling price. Is there enough margin after shipping, duties, and fees?
  4. Competition assessment — How many sellers already offer this on your target channels?
  5. Regulatory check — FDA, labeling requirements, ingredient restrictions for your market.
  6. Shipping logistics — Weight, fragility, shelf life. Can you import it efficiently?
  7. Brand differentiation — What makes your version different from existing options?
  8. Capital available — Do you have the capital to fund the initial order?
  9. Timeline — How long from order to shelf? Will the trend still be active?
  10. Exit strategy — If it doesn’t sell, can you liquidate without a total loss?

Adjusting the Formula

  1. Go to Formula Studio (Owner only).
  2. Adjust job weights or signal weights. Changes are saved immediately.
  3. Click “Recompute All Scores” to see the impact across all products.
  4. If scores look wrong, revert the weights. The previous values are logged.
Best practice: Make one change at a time. Adjust a single weight by 5%, recompute, and evaluate the impact before making another change.

Activating New Scrapers

To activate a platform that’s currently “pending” (YouTube, X, Facebook, Walmart, Etsy, Alibaba, Pinterest):

  1. Ensure the required API credential is set in agents/.env
  2. Set the platform’s schedule to enabled in the schedules table
  3. Update the scraper registry entry to “active” status
  4. Trigger a manual run to test before the nightly pipeline picks it up

Deploying to the Cloud

  • Railway or Render — Deploy the FastAPI server + scheduler. Estimated cost: $5–15/month.
  • Dashboard — Already deployed on Vercel (free tier).
  • Database — Supabase free tier handles current volume. Upgrade if you exceed 500MB.

Backup and Recovery

  • Supabase automatically backs up the database daily (paid plan). On the free plan, use Supabase’s point-in-time recovery.
